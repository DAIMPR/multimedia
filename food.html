3 classes of food

  1. Carbohydrates
  2. Protein
  3. Fats

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are polyhydroxy aldehydes, ketones, alcohols, acids, their simple derivatives and their polymers having linkages of the acetal type.

  • milk
  • potatoes
  • oats
  • kidney beans
  • chick peas

protein

Proteins are large biomolecules and macromolecules that comprise one or more long chains of amino acid residues.

  • beans
  • beef
  • milk
  • oats
  • eggs

Fats

Fat is a kilojoule-dense nutrient needed for energy and to help absorb vitamins A, D, E and K12

  • Butter
  • Dark chocolate
  • Lard
  • Fatty fish
  • Goose fat
